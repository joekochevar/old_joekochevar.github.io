Mechanical :

In addition to the projects I do for class, I am involved in several clubs and organizations revolving around mechanical engineering projects. I have several years of rigorous training with SolidWorks (including SolidWorks PDM), and have a fair amount of experience using Autodesk Inventor and PTC Creo. Furthermore, I have hands-on fabrication experience in the shop on an assortment of machinery, and a small amount of experience TIG welding aluminum.

Phoenix Racing
Ever since I arrived at Olin, I've been a dedicated member of the Olin College SAE Mini Baja team: Phoenix Racing. We're a team of over thirty students, building small off-road racing vehicles. Our team goals are to run a successful, large-scale engineering project, plug new students into this long-term, iterative project, and operate with financial and technical independence. Every year, we compete in multiple competitions against hundreds of other colleges from around the world, and we actually do pretty well. Currently, I am devoting almost all of my extracurricular time to the Baja team.

Olin Blind Sailing Research Team

I spent the summer before my senior year doing research with the olin college blind sailing research team. The goal of the project was to outfit a sailboat with sensors and equipment in order to allow blind sailors to sail independently of sighted guides.

The project was an attempt to allow blind sailors to race in the Paralympics: an opportunity denied to them due to their reliance on sighted sailors as guides during their races. It was also an effort to give blind sailors more independence.

REVO: Research of Electric Vehicles at Olin

Along with Baja, I have also spent a considerable amount of time working with the REVO club at Olin: a relatively young club that focuses on designing and building electric vehicles of various types. In REVO, I gained experience both using SolidWorks and in the machine shop. 

Specifically, I worked on REVO's electric racing cart, concentrating on the chassis subsystem. I redesigned and optimized the Ackermann Steering model by manipulating and refabricating individual steering components. I later also designed the seat mounting system to maintain a correctly-positioned center of gravity and ensure driver safety.

Dorm room second floor
My sophomore year, my roommate and I managed to lay claim on one of the only rooms in the school with ceilings twice as high as normal. To take advantage of our situation, I designed and built a second floor covering half of our room. It was designed to maximize space within the room, provide more storage room, and leave one large wall open as a projection space.

The loft was designed and tested using SolidWorks, and adhered to all fire codes and safety standards. It also featured a modular design, allowing it to be assembled, disassembled, or rearranged in a matter of minutes.

FIRST Robotics: team 2035

I spent four years as a dedicated member of the FIRST Robotics Team 2035. In doing so, I gained a deep initial look at systems integration and design, as well as extensive training on a variety of machines and software packages. I also learned how to TIG weld aluminum to make the frame for our various robots.

I spent time on several different subsystems, but focused primarily on the chassis and manipulator. While a lot of my designs were created ahead of time, much of my work involved rough, expedited FMEA and rapid redesign and fabrication of parts that failed at competition.

Picture-Based Digital Encryption System
A couple of years back, I had a project really take off, in the form of a Java-based digital encryption system. My concept relied on the near infinite variability of images as a source for encryption algorithms, and proposed that the next stage of encryption systems could be based on more organic forms of inspiration, making them possibly harder to crack.

I gave a TEDx presentation on my findings, as seen to the left. My final system was a self-contained Java-based software package that would parse three user-chosen images in order to encrypt or decipher text-based messages.
