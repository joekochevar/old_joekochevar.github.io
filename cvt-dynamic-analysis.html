CVT  Dynamic Analysis
In one of my mechanical engineering physics classes, Dynamics,  we were asked to pick a fairly complex physical system, and accurately simulate it in MATLAB to generate useful information. To continue with one of my usual themes, I chose to analyze the CVT with two other students, and use our simulation to aid in my tuning efforts on the Olin Baja team.

My analysis project began with disassembly, measurement, and analysis of each individual portion of the CVT to obtain important information, and ended with a MATLAB software package that allows us to simulate the shifting of our Gaged GX-9 CVT throughout a race on the Olin experimental racing track. This allows us to tune the CVT as we see fit, whether it's giving us the most jump off of the line,  the highest top speed, or a balance between the two.
