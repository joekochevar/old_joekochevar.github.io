Spaghetti﻿ Bridge
Our mid-semester project for my Mechanics of Solids and Structures class was building bridges out of strands of dry spaghetti. The objective of the project was to not only build a structurally stable bridge capable of holding up a significant point load, but also to predict the failure-causing load and position of failure.

My team designed and fabricated a bridge with an intentional failure point at about one hundred pounds of weight. Our design was completely aimed at minimizing buckling within the spaghetti columns, and force entirely compression or tension failure. Our design process also used materials science and stress tests in order to predict our bridge's failure point.

The final bridge was fabricated using 3d printed molds to ensure consistent results, had the highest strength-to-weight in the course, and an actual failure within .3% of our predicted failure load.
